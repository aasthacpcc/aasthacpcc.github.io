# Color Theory Project – Web Design Assignment

---

## **Objective**

Learn how to apply color theory in web design by creating harmonious, accessible color palettes and seeing them applied in interactive web components.

---

## **Instructions**

### **Part 1 — Explore Color Theory**

1. Review the **interactive sections** in the template:

   * **Hero Section** – Introduction to color theory.
   * **Interactive Palette Section** – See 4 pre-defined palettes applied to sample elements.
2. Understand how different combinations affect:

   * Readability
   * Visual appeal
   * Mood and tone

---

### **Part 2 — Create Your Palettes**

1. Use **Coolors.co** or **Adobe Color** to create **3 of your own palettes**.
2. Each palette should include:

   * 3 colors (HEX or RGB)
   * Harmony type (Complementary, Analogous, Triadic, Monochromatic, etc.)
   * Rationale: Why you chose these colors and how they could be used in a webpage.

---

### **Part 3 — Apply Your Palettes**

1. Use the **interactive buttons** to preview how pre-made palettes look.
2. Replace the placeholder colors in the **palette preview** and **example card** if desired.
3. Make sure your combinations maintain **high contrast for accessibility**.

---

### **Part 4 — Submit Your Work**

1. Fill in the **submission table** in the template:

   * Palette #
   * Colors (HEX or RGB)
   * Harmony type
   * Rationale
2. Optionally, you can modify the **example card** to create a small visual mockup using your palette.

---

## **Tips for Students**

* Focus on **contrast and readability** for web text and buttons.
* Consider **how colors work together** for headings, paragraphs, and UI elements.
* Use the interactive preview to **see real-time effects** of color choices.
* Document your reasoning clearly in the table and optionally in HTML comments.

---

## **Deliverables**

* Completed **index.html** with interactive palette section.
* Optional edits to **style.css** for custom palettes.
* Completed **submission table** with your 3 palettes and explanations.

---

This assignment helps students **practice selecting harmonious colors**, understand accessibility, and see **instant visual feedback** in a real web environment.
